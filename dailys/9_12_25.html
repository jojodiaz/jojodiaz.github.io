Day 1

Day 1. Day 1 of what? Day 1 of learning something every day, silly! Writing this in vim. Man. What a pain. Now don’t get me wrong — I love the gushing feeling of elitism you get when you write something in vim, but let’s be honest: most development goes way faster when you drop the pretentiousness and use VS Code like a normal person.

But the point of these daily notes is to:

  • Develop proficiency in tools I find cool/important
  • Have a written record of my growth as an engineer and as a person

Suffering through vim is just one step of the process. It feels right. So I created my .vimrc today with some small but insanely important commands: 

set tabstop=4
set number=on
syntax on

The next thing I want to do is make the styling for this page a separate CSS file. Modularity and all. But boy, do I feel negatively about already refactoring code. Yes, I understand technical debt build-up, but in true engineering fashion I am simply writing more and more code on top of it all.

But now I’m thinking of the recruiters that might read some of these pages. ****. ****! Fine, my next steps will be to beautify… AND modularize my crappy code. Blegh.
